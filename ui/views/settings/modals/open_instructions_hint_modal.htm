Using Open-Ended Questions to Evaluate Nominations
Open-ended questions provide an opportunity for you to share your insights and perspectives on each nomination. Please follow these simple instructions to effectively use open-ended questions in your evaluation:

Read the Nomination Materials:

  • Thoroughly review the nomination form, letters of support, and any additional documents provided for each nominee.

Understand the Question:

  • Carefully read and understand what each open-ended question is asking. These questions aim to gather your qualitative feedback and observations about the nominee's accomplishments, impact, and strengths.

Be Specific and Concise:

  • Provide clear, concise, and specific responses. Use examples from the nomination materials to support your points whenever possible.
  • Avoid vague or general statements. Focus on the unique qualities and achievements of the nominee.

Address Both Strengths and Areas for Improvement:

  • If applicapable, highlight the nominee's strengths and what sets them apart in their field.
  • If applicable, constructively discuss areas where the nominee could improve or where their nomination materials could be strengthened.

Use the Provided Text Box:

  • Type your response directly into the text box provided for each open-ended question.
  • Ensure your response fits within any specified word or character limits.

Review Your Responses:

  • Before submitting your evaluation, proofread your responses for clarity, grammar, and spelling.
  • Make sure your answers accurately reflect your assessment of the nomination.

By following these instructions, you will provide valuable qualitative feedback that enriches the evaluation process. Thank you for your thoughtful consideration and time in reviewing the nominations.
